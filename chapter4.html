Chapter 4:

The Fight to Undermine

By Maxa Millennial

The Fight to Undermine

By Maxa Millennial

Your psychic waves do not destroy me any longer. They do not attack me as they once did for now I sit sterile. My state has shifted into a calloused mind, protected from all around me, with only the dry splitting of my skin. I once believed I needed to remove myself from your presence, but now I realize that movement was one too far. Simply, I must remove myself, violently. I must embrace the barbs into my heart, plunge them deeper as I look into your eyes, feel the final moment between us. Only then will my transformation be complete. Only then will I come back as the shadow I always was.

But what does this mean for you and me? It means the guard will always stand, vigilant they must remain, for the moment we fall your hidden stiletto will pierce my very being, and I fear on that occasion all life will be drained forever.

Still, confusingly, I search for your motivations, and I search for my responsibility in the mire of our battered conversations. Perhaps it was my gaze, my moods, my stature, my facade of confidence, but the grotesque pleasure you take in my failure burns the tethers that connect us. Yet I still want to understand. I wonder who you are even as your scope is set to kill.

And who will win this sick game? I will, for my opponent does not lose because they do not play. And after I bask in the victory I will admonish myself alone on my pedestal. I will see myself for all it is and I will see nothing. I'll claim that nothing and take it with me into the ether, because I still remember you, and I still remember the butcherings we once took part in, a butchering I became accustomed to, a butchering you took pleasure in. Except, finally, us butchers have grown older, and, while I sit here in pieces, your gore induced sculpture, you will soon see the indivisible self. It won’t be yours, and you’ll wonder if you ever had one at all.

Of course then, unbethoughten to all of you, my return will not be felt as the warm confidant it once was. It will be the dark sparrow, the bright scream, the infinite terror, for you will not be able to escape these prisons you've constructed. You will sit for all eternity asking the questions that you pressed onto me as an eviscerator of self. And I will have nothing to say for I’ve been dead forever.

Come now, into my torture chamber, did you think this was just a ride you would soon finish with a swift return to your placated life?
