A Terminal Diagnosis

By Maxa Millennial

A Terminal Diagnosis

By Maxa Millennial

I've contracted the plague. This plague is not a fierce ailment of the body. It's not a brief snatching of life in a moment of agony or pain. It's worse than a simple, pleasurable second of bodily undoing. The plague I've contracted is a creeping destruction of the mind. It's an oozing crushing, a slow, methodical unraveling, an impossible to escape, leaving no survivors behind, force of domination. It's a pestilence that sweeps everything in its wake leaving a desolate landscape of sameness and death.

I wondered if a diagnosis of impending doom would change our life.

It changed mine. Except it didn't change it into one of poetic, cathartic experience. Instead, it simply forced me to observe its frivolousness.

As we fade from view and refuse to accept it, one sees there is no cure, and yet I probe every facet of my life.
