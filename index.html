Finding Zen

I wish I could put down the pen, find my zen, and then live a life with no price.

But instead the price has been set too high. It's a price I can pay only in the moment I die.

And so I'm a left with a debt, one I can never forget, one that sits in my mind, one I'll fear until the end of time.

And so I wait, waiting to accept my fate, hoping to accept my life, dreaming all the while.

A dream and fever, the fever of thought, the words come slow, the breath now too.

Oh God, my God, please tell me what to do.
Into the Gray

I expected to be marching on the Greener Side for a while now.

I thought my brain would hold me there until

It successfully found a way to rewire itself.

To remove the pangs of mundane feelings

I thought it would take years.

But the tyrannical forces of explosive emotion are here.

Into the Gray we go, alone.

There's space for another, but it's only me now.

The vacancy in my mind will soon be filled,

The connections I've forged soon to dilapidate.

The life I live to be unknowable.

The life you live, soon to be unknowable.

Or perhaps rather, it's into the black, into the deep, into the dark, into my mind. It attacks me. It tries to paralyze my thoughts.

When will my mind finally accept our fate.

1 year? 3? 30? A lifetime.

Why can't I let this life go?

And live without the fear.
Demolition Derby: HOUSE FOR SALE

There's that weight, back in the chest, except this one feels different from the rest.

Or maybe it's just evolving, because I know he has to take it back.

When a person leaves for only a week, their house begins to gather dust and the life fades out of it

Week two and one starts to inspect the foundation, realizing that patch after patch and fix after fix leave a place that can be toppled with the gust of a wind.

Week three and the heavens declare the house abandoned. The sad sight of a gathering area, full of emotion and fear, has been departed and left for me to clear.

Because my mind has yet to become a memorial, soon I'm sure it will, but until then the figures who've departed must be demolished.

The Greener Side - Part One

Feelings surface without my understanding.

Did I create this? And what if they're unprofessed, unexplained, and forever unheard?

Does an abrupt solitude harden the heart?

There's a weight over my shoulders today

It presses my hands down

It makes each stroke of the key feel like a final breath

But no one's present; no one can hear the thoughts rushing

This, is it the identity crisis I anticipated

This, is it the chemical imbalance I've long awaited

This, are they the one I don't want to let go

Wait, let me wander through the world as a nomad of our kind

Each path offers an eternal rest

So I'll choose to persist

I need to numb my mind, but my immunity is too strong for me
The Greener Side - Part Two

My small room is filled with gloom

My arms cause me alarm

Even my feet somehow deplete

And as I lay, I inspect, analyze, and find all the future pieces of my demise

How can one put a soul on a page?

Still then, it's just another cage.

I build my life on emptiness.

Walking ahead, head kept down with eyes up

My undermine: anger rises in me.

Now that I have a perfect measure, it's one that gives me no pleasure.

In the moments between, I fantasize about that alternate reality

The one where I'm high all the time, the one where I'm happy and can't figure out why

The Greener Side - Part Five

It's not just the weight that I feel in my chest

It's not a weight that's any different from the rest

But it's the weight that pushes my mind

It presses my psyche down depleting my thoughts and leaving nothing left

I can't play a song, I can't stretch a muscle, I can't respond to a text, I can't do what I'm supposed to do next.

I guess I need to get up, otherwise this life will become one that I despise.

It already is

Does writing anything do anything

Does manufacturing a feeling make me feel

Am I just missing the high that I've used to stabilize my whole life

It's never a good sign when I just want to fall asleep, when I just want to end my day, when I have to write anything down because I'm afraid I have nothing left to say
End Days

I will destroy myself. The battle to survive exists securely in my mind.

If you can't move beyond, if you can't take the leap into the abyss that is life, then you'll never live.

You'll wait and wait, hope and hope, until finally all those macabre dreams of self-pity are undeniably true.

There's nothing else to read, only time left to dread. Or spend?

Down the spiral we go once again.

Just one click away from sending my life astray.

These self-tests I perform only leave me forlorn.

For a life unlived, and with so much left to give.

Alas, as soon as I solve a problem I've created another.

But this time it's the wrist that causes my life to twist.
Moments In-between

Each day is accompanied by a few brief moments of existence. These are the Moments where I must trudge forward my will, for my will, my closest ally and confidant, cannot understand what we must do.

For a few brief moments, I must dredge myself from the pits of noxious gas. Standing at the edge, in the next painless step, I find myself at the bottom of my psyche, at the doorway to hellish destruction, or is this heaven?

For a few brief moments, I must convince myself I'm not special enough to die just yet. I must convince myself that I will persist, at my protest, to see myself continue on.

For a few brief moments, I conquer the world.
My Existential Romance

She understands my ambition for life but I am not my ambition.

She understands my role as a lover, but, sadly, I am not a lover.

She understands my eyes, my hands, lips, and my touch, but I am none of these things.

She understands everything about me, and yet, everything about me is not me.

She tries to understand what I cannot; it's the nothingness under it all.

She tries to understand; she understands nothing.
The Outside Witness

I can witness a friendship beginning to die.

The separation of state is too much for our feeble lives

If just one of us had the strength, a life of platitude could be made

But instead you're gone, and instead I wait, watching the buds of a depressed love trudge their way toward self-suicide

A plant that now refuses the sun so it eats its own flesh

But to its surprise the flesh of its own provides a new energy

Its roots are ripped from the ground only to show that these roots mean nothing to the life of a user

The life of an abuser.

I refuse, but my thoughts push on.
The Reasons I Can't Find

For some reason, what reason, a reason I can't find, the mere thought of you causes me to simmer

It causes me to stew, smolder, prickle, and retreat and somehow you're unware of my defeat

A part of me thinks, hopes, that ignoring it will cause that feeling to fade

But then a text tells me you've forgotten it all and I know it will never abate

Certainly, this is merely just the dramatist coming to life

But it gives me great pain to realize you've felt none of this strife

For how could you, when at the nearest sight of pain you've run away and hidden under some other's wing

And I've stepped forward, stayed, and gazed at my life of chosen malaise

Like a wound, your name begins to fester

I scratch at our words trying to find what's underneath

Just go away, I tell these remnants of you

Sometimes my whole essence sits alone in the universe

I wish I could reach out to you, but I know the comfort it might bring me isn't true

You're a lie I tell myself to feel okay

One that I need but one that can't stay

And worst of all, it's caused me to miss a day, to miss an hour that won't return, to see in me the things I never want to be but am; I must run away before you steal my soul.

Moments of vulnerability creep in broad daylight.

They stalk beside as one feels their self growing ever stronger.

They invert that strength leaving a so-called crumbling foundation.

One that the mind must either discard or callous over.

I dream of your smile

Awake and find you were never there

Awake to realize you never cared

I must convince myself that somethings gone horribly awry.

I must convince myself to convince myself that it's just my mind playing tricks on me.

These tricks involve my death a thousand times. It's a sapping of my life, the destruction of my body, and the forgotoness of my soul.

I must convince myself that these are only tricks.

I must convince myself that even death doesn't stick.
And, Today

And, today I wake up with the thought, 'This could be the final morning, the final afternoon, the final day where that terminal force is absent from my life.'

This is the thought he can't escape. It's the one that stems so deep his other, surface-level thoughts take on its contents without him even noticing. In an invisible struggle, he looks towards the others, any other, to see if, to try to observe how they stand against the forces that pull him inwards. Does their mind track the moments from one resignation to the next?

And, today I'm forced to ask myself, 'Was I wrong yesterday, to think that it could all come crashing to an end because it didn't?'

He claims the answer is 'no', a scalding 'no' that brands his thoughts with the knowledge that the ink is nearly set. An inescapable 'no' which forces his mind to continuously realize that, without a doubt, and without any possibility of transcendence, one must write the final page of their book. They must compose that final sentence, select the punctuation marking the permanence that is the end, all while wondering what would occur if they tried to turn the page.

When I think about him now, all I see is a trapped mind gifted with the knowledge that it won't happen to him but cursed with the everlasting thought that it should.

And, today I'm filled with hundreds of solemn pauses, between all matter of moments, where I ask, 'What will I have to resign this time?'

It's a question he must answer when he wakes up and when he goes to sleep. It's in his mind as he speaks to the co-worker and again while he's out at the cinema, interrupting him when he least expects it and then again when he most fears it. It does not cease at request or demand; it remains always, resolutely, at his side and in his thoughts.

And, at the end of a tired day, that question searches for my pulse one last time, until I drift to sleep, wondering if I'll have an answer tomorrow.
