Chapter 3:

Ego Death

By Maxa Millennial

Ego Death

By Maxa Millennial

With a brief glance at who I was supposed to be, I just try to ESCAPE. Along the way, I realize I've been brewing poisons and administering them to myself as the cure. And while these glances only catch their moment between the hard-battled life of substance-infused bliss, they come from a broken imagination that presents infinite possibility in the place of cold reality. Inside I take flight but out there I'm nowhere.

Within that empty place, it's a necessity that I interact with drug users. It's necessary because if faced with the dilemma of buying drugs, and by drugs I mean the most mundane substance in the escapist's toolkit, then one has to not only contact someone from that group, but they must also form a relationship. Every purchase first involves a social interaction in which the vocabulary is constantly being adapted to suit the character of the drugged out lollard. I actually like that part but, I've found, I don't share much with the underside even though I'm one of their usual suspects.

There is one commonality between us though. It's the usual refrain among the acidic, shroomed, and tripified ones: the ever enlightening Ego Death. 'I saw the world as though my individual self wasn't a part of it.' This phrase always pulls me in, but I quickly find I have little patience for these stories that almost all begin with the same, at this point, comical phrase, 'I forgot most of the trip, but,' but what they remember from the rest is surely an experience no one else has had before — minus their tripping friends of course. 'I forgot most of the trip, but what I remember is that it changed my life forever.'

So what's an ego death? According to the trippers it's a momentary lapse of worth, a span of time where every care and worry you have fades away, every love and hate fades away, every goal and ambition fades — quite a description, and it does the job well enough. Although, they really should use more precise language as their looseness mischaracterizes the lasting impression of the feeling that was felt. Mainly because at the end of each of those faded emotions is their revival. In their dive emotion departs, but then it returns.

Me? I've actually experienced an ego death; I killed it myself.
