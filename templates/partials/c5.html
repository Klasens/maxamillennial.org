My Brothers and Sisters

My Brothers and Sisters

By Maxa Millennial

And for you, my brothers and sisters, the kindred souls that I only know through the pain.

You, hold the line! The enemy that approaches is unseen. The enemy that approaches takes everything from you by telling you that there was nothing to possess.

You, stand tall! The unwinnable moment has arrived. The fear is at your feet.

You, look ahead! In this moment that is yours, you will have only an instant to glimpse your identity, and it will bring you nothing but dread.

You, do not cease! Your continuation is all that matters. Not your continuation of body or mind, but your continuation of soul. It matters not what the scorecard shows because don't you see you created it yourself. It only matters that you did not crumple up your creation and forget about the game you played.

You, embrace my hand! We stand together for as long as we have time. In this pit of despair you will soon leave me, and I will soon leave you. And yet, in this moment we create. You and I, a universe as our universe.

You, strike the iron! Do not let your temperature drop. It must remain hot if we are to gaze the finish line. The shivering of your soul is not a death throe, it’s merely a reflex. This is not your time. Your time will never come on this day. Your time will be with me for all eternity.

You, it’s okay now, it’s over. Your valiant charge into the dust has caused quite a stir, but like all things you and I will soon settle now. We can finally settle into the nothingness we’ve known but never seen, pursued as we sprinted away from. This life, existence, we’ve netted is just like the rest, and still so different, and it’s ours.

You, me.
