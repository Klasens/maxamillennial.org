A Journal Entry from the Last Man on Earth

By Maxa Millennial

A Journal Entry from the Last Man on Earth

By Maxa Millennial

I must say I'm quite surprised to come down from this high and find you all gone. In the duration of the brief few years I've spent in the fog, the populations that surrounded me have disappeared. Now that I stand alone, it's as though I have nothing left to say or nobody left to say it to. I suppose my final task will be to excavate downward, to dig deeper, to catalog the destructions that have been brought upon our world, destructions that have apparently struck you all.

But I must confess, now that it's just me, there's a certain joy I plan to take. A joy that comes with the knowledge of a humanity departed and dead. A joy that stems outward from, well, to be coy, pure dread.

Because with that joy, the evil that strangles our deepest mind can harden our hearts into diamonds. Because with that joy, the evil that decomposes the living can fertilize the flowers of beautiful doom.

Because only when the death that surrounds us all is cultivated and curated can the spotlight of proliferation be switched on and directed.

But where do I shine the light? I think the only option that remains is to point upward, like a beam shooting out from the center of our Earth, and wait for the next set of beings to discover the pages of my final days.
