A Meeting

By Maxa Millennial

A Meeting

By Maxa Millennial

When I asked him what genre he wrote in, he looked at me, smirked, and said "Everything except comedy".

From the moment we met him, his eyes always probed the conversation. It was like there were two interactions happening simultaneously: His words spoke a message while his eyes keenly surveyed each participant. They had a grayish hue causing his gaze to splay a feeling of overcast across each string of phrases. It had the effect of producing a fleeting darkness that overtook every thought just as it fizzled out.

Throughout Maxa Millennial's existence, he strove to write thousands of pages proclaiming each page created an everlasting bond between reality and existence. Yet all that he could bear to manage are the few works found here. He wrote with a purpose that he knew had no method of prolonged existence. Listening to what read as confessions, one can feel a sense of relief as his demons, imagined and invented, envelop the light around him.

But now, with his death plainly passed, a post-mortem can finally be performed. It begins with a sweeping surge of drama -- melodrama that is. For when one traces the journey of Maxa Millennial, what's found is a life twisted by apocalyptic thoughts, an existence with the extreme always in reach, with the next moment always in question.

Perhaps it's because, if he forgot this truth, if he forgot where he came from, he would never remember where he needed to go.
